â— Why Bullying Matters

Bullying can hurt people for a long time, not just for one day. It can make someone feel sad, scared, or lonely, and it can affect how they learn and make friends.

Sometimes, the hurt feelings stay even after the bullying stops. This can make a person lose confidence, feel worried, or find it hard to trust others. Bullying can also make school and other places feel unsafe and unhappy.

ğŸ“Š Types of Effects

Bullying can hurt how a person feels inside. When someone is bullied again and again, they may feel worried, stressed, or scared, and not feel safe.

Bullying can also make a person feel bad about themselves and start to doubt their abilities. Over time, it can make them feel very sad or afraid, and stop them from feeling happy and confident.

Bullying can make it hard for students to learn. When someone is being bullied, they may feel worried or distracted, so it is difficult to pay attention in class.

This can lead to lower grades and not wanting to join lessons or activities. Sometimes, students may even avoid coming to school because they are afraid, which can affect their learning even more.

Bullying can damage a person's social relationships and sense of belonging. Victims may withdraw from peers and experience isolation, feeling excluded or unwanted.

Trust in others can be lost, especially if the bullying comes from classmates or friends. As a result, individuals may find it difficult to form or maintain friendships, which can affect their social development and confidence in group settings.
